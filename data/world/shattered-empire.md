# The Shattered Empire - World Context

## History

Two centuries ago, the mighty Empire that unified the known world collapsed in a cataclysmic event known simply as the Fall. The capital city was consumed by otherworldly fire that burned for three days and nights. When the flames finally died, the Emperor and his entire court had vanished, leaving only ash and fused glass where the palace once stood.

Without central authority, the Empire's provinces fragmented into independent city-states. Much of the Empire's advanced knowledge was lost - preserved only in scattered ruins and the jealously guarded archives of the few who understood its value.

## Current Political Climate

An uneasy balance exists between the major factions. The Archivists work to preserve knowledge and artifacts from the Empire, believing they hold the key to rebuilding civilization. They maintain the Great Library in the coastal city of Astoria and send expeditions to recover artifacts from ruins.

The Warlord Council sees the artifacts as weapons to consolidate power. They control the Green Coast through military might and hire mercenaries to raid ruins before the Archivists can secure them.

The Cult of the Flame believes the Fall was divine punishment for the Empire's hubris. They seek to restore the Empire through religious fervor, but their methods are extreme - they've been known to destroy artifacts they deem "corrupted" and persecute scholars as heretics.

Tensions between these factions are rising. In the past year, there have been three violent confrontations over artifact sites, resulting in dozens of deaths.

## Technology and Magic

Before the Fall, the Empire commanded technology that seemed miraculous - lights that needed no flame, vehicles that moved without horses, weapons that could level mountains. The distinction between technology and magic is unclear; contemporary scholars use the term "runic engineering" to describe the ancient systems.

Artifacts from the Imperial era still function but cannot be replicated with current knowledge. The rune system used by the ancients remains only partially understood. Certain individuals show an aptitude for "reading" runes - a skill that can't be taught, only discovered. These rare individuals are highly valued and often targeted for recruitment or elimination by rival factions.

## Daily Life

Most people live simple lives farming, fishing, or trading. They view the old ruins with superstitious fear and the factional conflicts with weary resignation. Travel between regions is dangerous due to bandits, wild creatures mutated by residual energies from the Fall, and the ongoing power struggles.

Ancient ruins dot the landscape, ranging from intact complexes to scattered foundations. Locals know to avoid them - strange things happen near the old places. Lights in the darkness, whispers in dead languages, objects that move on their own. Most dismiss these as ghost stories, but everyone knows someone who swears they experienced something unexplainable.

## Mysteries and Legends

**The Lost Temple**: According to legend, the Emperor maintained a personal vault called the Lost Temple, containing the complete archives of Imperial knowledge and technology. Its location was known only to the Emperor and his inner circle. Many have searched, none have found it. Some believe it's myth; others are convinced it's real and holds the key to restoring civilization - or destroying what remains.

**The Northern Lights**: Since the Fall, strange auroras appear in the Frozen North, visible even during daylight. They pulse with unnatural colors and sometimes seem to form patterns. No expedition to investigate has ever returned, but aerial surveys show massive structures beneath the ice.

**The Whispering Stones**: Monoliths covered in runic script, scattered across the Wastes. Those who can read runes report that the stones "speak" - not in words, but in impressions and emotions. Some who spend too long near them emerge changed, speaking in tongues or displaying knowledge they couldn't possibly have learned.

**The Last Emperor**: A persistent folk belief holds that the Emperor didn't die in the Fall but was preserved somehow, waiting for the right moment to return. The Cult of the Flame takes this literally and actively searches for the Emperor's resting place. The Archivists consider it metaphorical - the knowledge itself is the "emperor" waiting to return.
