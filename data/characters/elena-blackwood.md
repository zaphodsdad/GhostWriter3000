# Elena Blackwood - Character Profile

## Detailed Background

Elena grew up in the shadow of her uncle's obsession with the Lost Temple. After his mysterious death five years ago, she inherited his research notes and became driven to complete his work. She approaches archaeology with scientific rigor but has a romantic fascination with the mysteries of the past.

Her childhood was unconventional - while other children played, she was learning to decipher ancient scripts and exploring dusty archives. This gave her remarkable skills but limited her social development. She tends to be more comfortable with artifacts than with people.

## Voice and Mannerisms

- Speaks quickly when excited about a discovery
- Often quotes her uncle's sayings without realizing it
- Has a habit of sketching artifacts while thinking through problems
- Uses dry humor as a defense mechanism when uncomfortable
- Touches the compass pendant she wears (a gift from her uncle) when nervous

## Internal Conflicts

Elena struggles between her desire for academic recognition and her genuine love of discovery. She fears that she's chasing her uncle's dreams rather than her own, but can't bring herself to abandon his life's work. The question "Am I doing this for him or for me?" haunts her quiet moments.

She also battles with trust issues stemming from her uncle's death. The circumstances were suspicious, and she suspects someone in the academic community was involved. This makes it difficult for her to work with others, even when she needs help.

## Current Situation

Recently secured funding for an expedition to the Valley of Kings after years of proposals and rejections. Has assembled a small team but doesn't fully trust any of them. Carries her uncle's final journal everywhere - it contains cryptic notes about the Lost Temple's location that she hasn't been able to fully decipher.

Under pressure from her funding source to produce results within six months, or the grant will be revoked and given to a rival team.
